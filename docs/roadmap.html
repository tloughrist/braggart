Braggart

Product roadmap · July 2026

From match tracker to convention companion

Braggart records tabletop games and turns the history into meaningful player rankings. The core app is built and tested in CI. Here is where it stands and the phases ahead.

North star A board-game convention as one group, every attendee a player
Near-term goal A live, polished portfolio demo

Where it stands today

Built, behind row-level security, and covered by pgTAP tests in CI.
Shipped A robust, viable app
✓Authentication with session handling and profiles
✓Groups with owner / admin / member roles
✓Match recording — individual and team, with handicaps and dates
✓History with filtering (game, date, players) and sorting
✓Tournaments — grouped matches, standings, end and delete
✓Group-scoped per-game leaderboards
✓Elo and Glicko-2 rankings, computed in Postgres
✓Networked compare-players across shared opponents
✓Avatar image upload and searchable pickers
✓Mobile-first responsive UI; single Expo codebase
In progress AWS web deployment. The CDK stack (private S3 behind CloudFront) synthesizes cleanly and is awaiting account activation. The 19-table schema already anticipates much of what follows.

The phases ahead

Two forces shape the order: the convention north star and default art assets in production now.
→ →

Near-term path for the portfolio demo: ship it, cash in the art with a recognition layer, then add the real-time flourishes that demo best — deferring the operational onboarding and deeper social work until a real convention is on the calendar.

Phase 1

Ship it & harden

Get it live on AWS and make it trustworthy. Foundation before features — a portfolio needs a live URL and a convention needs reliability.

  • AWS deployment (CDK stack is ready), plus observability, backups, and error monitoring
  • A final mobile-responsiveness pass
Near-term pathFoundation
Phase 2

Onboarding & scale

Make “a convention is one group” actually work: hundreds of strangers joining in minutes, on bad venue wifi.

  • Self-service join via group join code / QR and self-registration
  • A staff / moderator tier above admin for organizers
  • Searchable, paginated member lists and leaderboards
  • Offline-tolerant match capture with sync
Convention-critical
Phase 3

Recognition layer

Cash in the artist's assets. Highest delight-per-effort on the board, and time-sensitive to the art landing now.

  • Trophy and award UI — already modeled and seeded, no interface yet
  • Default asset packs (avatars, trophies, awards) wired in as the artist delivers
  • An awards-ceremony / end-of-event leaderboard — the celebratory payoff
Near-term pathArt-timed
Phase 4

Social graph

Connect players directly — the backlog's “big one.” Friendships also give the ranking graph real edges instead of group-inferred ones.

  • Friendships with a request / accept flow
  • Persistent teams, session notes, and mentions
Enriches rankings
Phase 5

Competitive structure

Organized play on top of the existing tournament base — not just a name, but who plays whom.

  • Structured tournaments: brackets, rounds, seeding, matchups
  • Live standings and printable brackets
Builds on tournaments
Phase 6

Discovery & real-time

Keep attendees in the moment — and the most demo-friendly layer for a live portfolio piece.

  • A “looking for players” / open-table board and a convention game library
  • Push notifications (match invites, “your round is up”)
  • Supabase-realtime live leaderboards
